Shengqin Zoo

Virtual Zoo of Animal Images in Chinese Traditional Culture

Artist: Wang Yifan

May, 2019

Introduction

Perhaps due to my fondness for pets, I have always been particularly interested in the study of animal imagery. During my three years of graduate study, I was deeply influenced by Professor Chen Nan's research and design practice on Chinese traditional design philosophy and symbol system, which led me to the idea of constructing an animal world in the context of Chinese traditional culture in my graduation design. This topic was supported and encouraged by Professor Chen. Through numerous discussions, the creative framework became clear. Animals not only have biological attributes, but are also an important part of the Chinese traditional cultural symbol system. Through sorting out the special topic of animals, a learning and interaction platform that is interesting for Chinese traditional culture can be designed.

Under the concept of "unity of man and nature" in traditional Chinese philosophy, animals and humans are seen as undifferentiated life. Not only in China, many ancient cultures contain images of humans combined with animals.

For example, Fuxi and Nuwa in ancient China, Anubis in ancient Egypt, and Pan in ancient Greece.

I collected more than 1000 images of animal themes and related text descriptions from traditional Chinese cultural art and selected over 300 images as initial samples to design a virtual zoo. The creative process went through several revisions and constant direction correction. Through discussions with my tutor, it gradually became clear that in the graduation design practice, the means and forms of design presentation should be combined with traditional reading media in a younger, more aesthetically pleasing and communicative interactive way.

The construction of the virtual zoo itself is a process of research and application of the visual system. The results can be interactively displayed, serving to popularize ancient Chinese animal images, and also combine design with cultural research, promoting Chinese culture and enhancing national confidence, which has social significance.
