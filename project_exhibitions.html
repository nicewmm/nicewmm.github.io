Verifying the Ancient and Understanding the Present: Oracle Bone Script Culture Exhibition

Supervisor: Chen Nan
Location: National Museum of China
December, 2019

This exhibition, commemorating the 120th anniversary of the discovery of oracle bone script, was held at the National Museum of China. Chen Nan's studio was responsible for the overall visual image design and contributed several pieces to the exhibition. I was involved in the design and planning work for the exhibition.

In this exhibition, we have recreated the multimedia immersive space experience 'Five Elements', based on the oracle bone script animation 'Sun Shooting', created by Professor Chen Nan in 2006.
The five groups of situational experiences are structured around the theme of the 'Five Elements' in traditional Chinese culture, where 'metal, wood, water, fire, and earth' respectively form the scenes of armed soldiers, lush forests, stormy lakes, tribal bonfires, and earthly creatures. These scenes are then woven into a complete story. Visitors can freely choose to experience different scenes through a touch screen. All graphic symbols in the work are composed of oracle bone script designed by Professor Chen.

Other Exhibitions

Supervisor: Chen Nan
2016-2022

During my master's and doctoral studies, I participated in many of Professor Chen Nan's exhibition designs. I was mainly responsible for the design of display boards and sometimes for communicating with production staff and overseeing installation work. The Oracle Bone script is very interesting, and Professor Chen's research is highly meaningful. I am honored to have been involved in these projects, and I have learned a lot through practice.
