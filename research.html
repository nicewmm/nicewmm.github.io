Intelligent Design Theory and Methods

Research on the Application and Influence of Artificial Intelligence in Visual Communication Design

Wang Yifan
Tsinghua University, 2024.

Abstract

This paper investigates the application and influence of Artificial Intelligence (AI), specifically deep learning neural networks, in the evolving field of visual communication design. It scrutinizes AI's current role in various areas, such as image generation, font design, logo design, and layout design, highlighting its capabilities to enhance work efficiency, automate complex design projects, and assist in trend exploration and decision-making. However, it also notes AI's limitations, emphasizing that it cannot replace human designers due to its inability to handle certain creative and practical aspects of design. Despite this, AI profoundly impacts design thinking, methods, and practice, and plays a significant role in the professional development of designers. The paper introduces three new AI-driven paradigms: brute-force design, fuzzy design, and ubiquitous design. In conclusion, the study emphasizes that understanding the implications of AI and maintaining a forward-looking perspective are essential for navigating the technological advancements in visual communication design.

Covered with AI: The Speculative Design

Wang Yifan, Jiang Xiangwei
Chinese Art, 2024, (03): 80-87.

Abstract

This article, through the comparison of real-world packaging, digital packaging, and AI packaging, discusses how different forms of packaging under different levels of human participation can trigger different feelings about the relationship between humans and objects, and humans and technology, thereby generating thoughts about the relationship between technological development and human values. This article focuses on the key role of design in promoting the reflection on the relationship between humans and objects, positions AI packaging as a kind of speculative design, and demonstrates through design practice the promotion of this new form of art on human thinking.

How Artificial Intelligence Affects Logo Design

Wang Yifan
Design, 2024, 37(09):19-21.

Abstract

Artificial intelligence is one of the most eye-catching developments in the field of technology in recent years. This article discusses the application of artificial intelligence in logo design and summarizes the ways and characteristics of AI-generated logos. These methods can improve the efficiency of logo design and facilitate public participation. However, at this stage, these methods cannot replace the professional level of experienced graphic designers. With the help of artificial intelligence, logo design has become simpler in some aspects, but it has also raised new issues, making logo design more complex.

Harmonizing with Machines: Development and Challenges of Human-Machine Collaborative Design

Wang Yifan
Design Research, 2024, (06)

Abstract

In the era of artificial intelligence, machines are no longer merely tools for executing tasks, but are increasingly venturing into creative domains of human beings, including the highly imaginative field of art design. This paper traces the three stages of machine involvement in art design: imitation, assistance, and creation. It then analyzes the current state of artificial intelligence participation in art design, producing a rich visual content through supervised and unsupervised learning. It also examines the uncontrollability of AI design, the lack of innovation, and the limitations of bias and averaging in AI-generated designs. Finally, the paper proposes the trend of co-design development involving human-machine collaboration, including the establishment of intelligent design standards, the creation of high-quality training samples, the expansion of various forms of human-machine communication, and the balance of responsibilities and rights between humans and machines.

Visualization of Dreams: An Analysis of Artificial Intelligence in Surrealist Art Creation

Zhao Xuanwu, Wang Yifan, Uradee Amnueypol, Yang Hong
19th International Conference on the Arts in Society in 2024 (Hanyang University, Seoul, Korea)

Abstract

As AI becomes integrated into artistic works, artists are increasingly focused on the interaction between AI and art forms. This article focuses on surrealism art, researching the expression of AI in surrealistic art creation through examination and comparison. In terms of visual effects, AI can imitate surrealistic works but lacks the expression of human emotions and individual will. This is the most essential difference from actual artist works. However, this research discovers three perspectives on the role of AI in surrealistic art creation aspects: 1. Offering a new mode of creative expression, AI enables humans to convey their imagination into visual form and express themselves through the rapid generation of visual images. 2. Introducing new forms of communication, as communicating with AI allows people to understand and imagine both reality and the surreal. 3. Serving as a new implementing tool, AI becomes an integral part of an artwork and presents the final form of art. This study aims to provide insights into surrealistic art creation in the new era.

Design Philosophy Under the Force of Technology: Materiality, Holisticness, Creativity

Wang Yifan
9th Oriental Design Forum 2023 (Shanghai Jiao Tong University, Shanghai, China)

Abstract

Design philosophy is the study of design thinking, design concepts, and design methods. The technological revolution has had a profound impact on design and also affects people's thinking about design philosophy. From the Industrial Revolution to the era of artificial intelligence, technology is forcing changes in design. By comparing with history, designers need to recognize the impact of technology from a macro perspective of design philosophy, think about the relationship between people and objects, the relationship between the inside and outside as a whole, and the relationship of human-machine creativity, in order to promote innovation and progress in the design field.

The Creativity of Machines and Its Application in Visual Communication Design

Wang Yifan
15th University Design Research Doctoral Forum in 2023 (Tsinghua University, Beijing, China)

Abstract

Creativity is considered one of the fundamental characteristics of human intelligence, and many scholars believe that machines lack creativity, which is a reproduction of existing content. With the continuous development of technology, a new viewpoint has emerged: artificial intelligence can generate creativity, but this is a kind of creativity different from humans. The creativity of machines is reflected in the process of interacting with people, prompting people to think about issues that they have not considered before, thereby enhancing human creativity. This kind of creativity has good applications in artistic creation, especially in visual communication design.

Research on Design Thinking and Methods Based on Chinese Character Stroke Order

Wang Yifan
Daguan(Forum), 2019, (07):44-46.

Abstract

The stroke order of Chinese characters is a combination of strokes and their sequence in Chinese characters. It is the process between information generation and information reception. It refers to both the order of single strokes and the sequence of multiple strokes. In addition, the term "stroke order" also means convenience and appropriateness, that is, using the stroke order norms can make writing more convenient. By exploring the definition, formation reasons, and related research of stroke order, the article attempts to extract new design thinking and methods, and apply them to new design activities.

Creating Cultural Sculptureï¼šAnalyzing the Value of Traditional Chinese Sculpture Based on the Five Levels of Human Cognition

Yang Hong, Amnueypol Uradee, Wang Yifan
The International Journal of Arts Theory and History, 2024, 18(2): 21-42.

Abstract

Sculptural art is the result of human mental activity, representing the materialization of human cognition. Its creation throughout history serves as a profound record of the evolving human mind and the characteristics of its time. In the field of cognitive science, Professor Cai Shushan classifies human cognition into five levels, including neural cognition, psychological cognition, linguistic cognition, thinking cognition, and cultural cognition. Based on the theory of five levels of human cognitive science, this article changes the conventional chronological approach, by exploring the relationship between the development of sculpture art and human consciousness, redraws the development sequence of traditional Chinese sculpture art, and analyzes the development form of sculpture art at different levels. The results reveal the importance for sculptors to improve their cultural awareness, which will guide sculpture art creation and sculpture education.

Study on the Observation Method of Animal Pictograms

Wang Yifan
Appreciation, 2019, (12): 302-303.

Abstract

Animal images widely appear in characters, and animal-themed oracle bone inscriptions occupy a large proportion of all oracle bone inscriptions that have been interpreted, most of which are pictographic characters. This article will categorize and organize the oracle bone inscriptions about animals that have been collected, and by analyzing the structure of these animal pictograms, try to speculate the different observation methods when our ancestors created characters, including observation angles, whole and part, dynamic and static, as well as observations of living environments, and interpret and gain insights from a design perspective.
